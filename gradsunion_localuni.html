Do you know that Singapore has 6 autonomous universities?

Singapore's university education aspires to prepare students not only for today’s world but also, for a world where jobs that have yet to be invented and challenges not yet foreseen. — MOE Website streetview

National University of Singapore (NUS)

NUS's admission, scholarships and more!

NUS has a semester-based modular system for conducting courses. It adopts features of the British system, such as small group teaching and course credits, and has 16 faculties, including Arts and Social Sciences, Business, Computing, Dentistry, Design and Environment, Engineering, Integrative Sciences and Engineering, Law, Medicine, Music, Public Health, Public Policy and Science. — NUS Wikipedia

Ranked 1st locally and 11th globally — QS World University Rankings 2019

Nanyang Technological University (NTU)

NTU's admission, scholarships and more!

NTU is organised into eight colleges and schools, including the College of Engineering, College of Science, Nanyang Business School, Lee Kong Chian School of Medicine, and College of Humanities, Arts and Social Sciences.It's also home to several autonomous institutions such as Singapore's National Institute of Education, S. Rajaratnam School of International Studies, Earth Observatory of Singapore, Singapore Centre on Environmental Life Sciences Engineering, Institute on Asian Consumer Insight, and the recently launched NTU Institute of Science and Technology for Humanity. — NTU Wikipedia

Ranked 2nd locally and 12th globally — QS World University Rankings 2019

Singapore Management University (SMU)

SMU's admission, scholarships and more!

SMU provides broad-based business programmes modelled after the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. It comprises six schools offering undergraduate, graduate, and PhD programmes in business administration, business analytics, financial services, accountancy, economics, information systems management, law and the social sciences. — SMU Wikipedia

Ranked 3rd locally and 500th globally — QS World University Rankings 2019

Singapore Institute of Technology (SIT)

SIT's admission, scholarships and more!

SIT offers industry-focused, applied degree programmes and confers its own degree programmes as well as specialised degree programmes with overseas universities. It's degree programmes are grouped into five clusters: Engineering (ENG), Chemical Engineering and Food Technology (CEFT), Infocomm Technology (ICT), Health and Social Sciences (HSS), and Design and Specialised Businesses (DSB). — SIT Wikipedia

Ranked 4th locally — QS World University Rankings 2019

Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD)

SUTD's admission, scholarships and more!

SUTD's collaboration with MIT covers development of the undergraduate curriculum, Co-teaching of subjects by MIT faculty, student exchanges, recruitment and development of SUTD faculty and establishment of a major co-located research center, SUTD–MIT International Design Centre (IDC). It also jointly partakes with Zhejiang University (ZJU) in research collaborations to address challenges associated with the world today, in areas such as healthcare, transportation, clean energy, and the environment. — SUTD Wikipedia

Ranked 5th locally — QS World University Rankings 2019

Singapore University of Social Sciences (SUSS)

SUSS's admission, scholarships and more!

SUSS focuses on applied degree programmes primarily in the social sciences. Its academia is organised into five schools, SR Nathan School of Human Development, School of Business, School of Humanities and Behaviourial Sciences, School of Law, School of Science and Technology, Institute for Adult Learning (IAL), College for Lifelong & Experiential Learning (CLEL), UC and five centres. — SUSS Wikipdia

Ranked 6th locally — QS World University Rankings 2019
